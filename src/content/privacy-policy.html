3. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

We may share your information with contracted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our business. 5. Data Security

We take the security of your personal information seriously. We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, use, alteration, or disclosure. These safeguards include:

  • Secure socket layer (SSL) encryption for data transmitted through our website
  • Secure payment processing through PCI-compliant providers (we do not store full payment card numbers on our systems)
  • Access controls limiting personnel who can view personal information to those with a legitimate business need
  • Password protection and, where available, multi-factor authentication on systems containing personal data
  • Confidentiality agreements with employees, contractors, and service providers who access personal information
  • Periodic review of our data security practices

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information using commercially reasonable means, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of any account credentials or booking references we provide to you.

5.1 Data Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach that compromises your personal information, we will:

  • Investigate and take steps to contain the breach promptly
  • Notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay, as required by Virginia law and other applicable regulations
  • Notify relevant regulatory authorities as required by law
  • Provide information about steps you can take to protect yourself
  • Take remedial measures to prevent future incidents

9. California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

9.1 Right to Know

You have the right to request that we disclose:

  • The categories of personal information we have collected about you
  • The categories of sources from which we collected personal information
  • Our business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling personal information
  • The categories of third parties with whom we share personal information
  • The specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you

9.2 Right to Delete

You have the right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions (such as completing transactions, detecting security incidents, complying with legal obligations, or exercising free speech rights).

9.3 Right to Correct

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.

9.4 Right to Opt Out of Sale or Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising purposes. Therefore, there is no need to opt out of sale or sharing.

9.5 Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information

We do not use or disclose sensitive personal information for purposes other than those permitted under the CCPA/CPRA (such as providing the services you requested).

9.6 Right to Non-Discrimination

We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights. We will not deny you services, charge you different prices, or provide you with a different level of service because you exercised your rights under the CCPA/CPRA.

9.7 Authorized Agents

You may designate an authorized agent to make requests on your behalf. We may require written proof of the agent's authorization and may still require you to verify your identity directly with us.

9.8 Financial Incentives

We do not offer financial incentives for the collection or sale of personal information.

10. Virginia Privacy Rights

If you are a Virginia resident, you may have additional rights under the Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA):

10.1 Your Rights

  • Right to Confirm: The right to confirm whether we are processing your personal data
  • Right to Access: The right to access the personal data we have about you
  • Right to Correct: The right to correct inaccuracies in your personal data
  • Right to Delete: The right to delete personal data you have provided or that we have obtained about you
  • Right to Portability: The right to obtain a copy of your personal data in a portable and, to the extent technically feasible, readily usable format
  • Right to Opt Out: The right to opt out of:
    • The processing of personal data for targeted advertising
    • The sale of personal data (we do not sell personal data)
    • Profiling in furtherance of decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects concerning you

10.2 Our Practices

We do not sell personal data. We do not engage in profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects concerning consumers. We do not process personal data for targeted advertising purposes based on data from third parties.

10.3 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise your Virginia privacy rights, please contact us using the information in Section 13. We will respond within 45 days as required by law. If we decline your request (in whole or in part), we will explain the reason. You may appeal a decision by contacting us within a reasonable period with a written explanation of your appeal. We will respond to appeals within 60 days.
